Definition of Terminologies Relevant to Malaria Parasites (3 of 4)

Description of Terms

The following terms are used in describing malaria and each species:

• Malaria derives its name from the Italian words mal aria, meaning "bad air" because malaria was associated with the unpleasant smell of marshes and swamps.

• Plasmodium falciparum derives its name from the Latin words falc = sickle, scythe + parum = bearing; thus, "bearing a scythe," a reference to the crescentic gametocytes. P. falciparum is the most lethal of the human malarias.

• Plasmodium vivax derives its name from its movement within erythrocytes seen in wet blood films.

• Plasmodium ovale derives its name from some of the infected erythrocytes which appear oval with extensions of the host cell membrane (fimbriations).
