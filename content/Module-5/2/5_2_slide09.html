Quality Indicators (6 of 7)

Proficiency Testing (PT)

The Proficiency Testing (PT) indicator is the percentage of correct PT results. In PT schemes, specimens with undisclosed results are provided by an external provider for analysis in your laboratory. Stained thick and thin blood films for detection, species identification and counting of malaria parasites are provided by such schemes.

Once you have examined the slides, you are required to submit your results to the PT provider for review and award of marks. Participation in PT schemes provides an evaluation of staff, methods, procedures and instruments. It also demonstrates competency, enables staff training, allows inter-laboratory measurement comparisons, facilitates process control and improves performance over time.

Client Satisfaction Surveys (CSS)

Client Satisfaction Surveys (CSS) address the percentage of clients satisfied with various aspects of the laboratory services such as confidentiality, courtesy, explanation of procedures, cleanliness, waiting time, turnaround time, result information and counselling.
