Document and Record Control Plan

Documents by definition require regular review and possible updating. It is therefore important to establish systems for managing documents so that current versions are always available. A document control system provides procedures for formatting and maintaining an inventory of documents and should include:

  • Maintaining a master list of all documents in the laboratory.
  • Ensuring that the most current version of any document is the one that is in use.
  • Ensuring availability and ease of use when a document is needed.
  • Providing for the appropriate archiving of documents when they need to be replaced.
  • Having a uniform format for documents that includes a numbering system and a method for identifying the version and date of the document.
  • Having a process for formal approval of documents, a distribution plan and a procedure for updating and revising documents.
