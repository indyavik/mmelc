Phases of Errors in Malaria Microscopy (2 of 4)

The following are some common errors that you may encounter in the malaria diagnostic laboratory in the three phases of the diagnostic cycle.

Pre-Examination Errors

Some examples of common pre-examination errors include:

  • Communication barrier, e.g. language, unconscious patient, cultural
  • Collecting the wrong specimen for testing or using the wrong specimen collection technique
  • Inadequate blood specimen
  • Blood specimen collected into the wrong tube or with the wrong anticoagulant
  • Mislabelling or failing to label the specimen
  • Misplacement of specimens upon reception
  • Storing blood specimens incorrectly prior to testing resulting in specimen deterioration, such as freezing
  • Improper transportation of blood specimens causing damage to blood cells or endangering staff and compromising public safety
  • Damaging test reagents or test kits by storing them improperly
