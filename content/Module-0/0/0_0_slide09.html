Course Structure (2 of 3)

The modules cover all important aspects of laboratory diagnosis of malaria including essential equipment, supplies and reagents; blood specimen collection; preparation and examination of blood films; recognition of malaria parasites; recognition of non-malaria pathogens and artefacts in stained blood films; reporting and interpretation of laboratory results; patient management based on laboratory test results; basic laboratory management; and quality assurance.

The course can be used for self-instruction, as a refresher course or as an instructor's manual. You can select individual modules as needed, or you can take the complete course from beginning to end. Each module ends with a quiz to help you review the information presented and assess your learning. Other suggested ways in which you can use the course include:

  • Working jointly with a colleague or in a group
  • Providing the course as a training tool for your national programme
