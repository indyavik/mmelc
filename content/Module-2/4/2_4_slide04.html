Staining Malaria Blood Films using 10% Giemsa Stain Solution

The rapid (10% Giemsa stain working solution) method is the most common method for staining 1â€•15 slides at a time. The rapid method is used in outpatient clinics and busy laboratories where a quick diagnosis is an essential part of case management.

This method is efficient, but more stain is used. The need for speed justifies the additional cost. Each blood film needs approximately 3 mL of stain to cover it. Therefore you need to prepare the volume that corresponds to the number of slides to be stained.

Staining Malaria Blood Films using 3% Giemsa Stain Solution

The slow (3% Giemsa stain working solution) method is used for staining larger numbers of slides (20 or more). It is ideal for staining blood films collected during cross-sectional or epidemiological surveys, field research or for preparing batches of slides for teaching.

This method is less appropriate when a quick result is needed. This method is less expensive than the rapid method because much less stain is used.
