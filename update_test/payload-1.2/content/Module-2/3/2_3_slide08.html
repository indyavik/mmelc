Procedure for Preparing Giemsa Stain Stock Solution

The procedure for preparing Giemsa stain stock solution involves the following seven steps:

Clean about 50 mL of glass beads in a small volume of absolute methanol in a beaker and place in a dark or amber bottle.

Weigh 3.8 g Giemsa stain powder on an analytical balance and pour into the bottle containing the glass beads using a funnel to avoid spillage.

Gently pour in about 100 mL of the absolute methanol ensuring all dry stain powder is washed into the bottle.

Tighten the screw cap on the bottle and shake in a circular motion for 2–3 minutes to start dissolving the stain crystals.

Using the funnel, add 250 mL glycerol to the mixture and repeat the shaking for 3–5 minutes.

Using the funnel, add the remaining 150 mL of absolute methanol to the mixture ensuring that the last of the methanol washes the last of the glycerol from the funnel into the stain mixture.


Tighten the screw cap on the bottle to prevent absorption of water vapour from the air and store in a cool dry place away from direct sunlight. Label the bottle clearly with the batch number, name of the person who prepared the stock solution and date of preparation. Continue shaking for 2–3 minutes about six times on the first day. Repeat the shaking every day (shake for 2–3 minutes about six times) for at least seven days. A shaker may be used, if available.
