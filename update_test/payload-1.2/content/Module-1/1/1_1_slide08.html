Local Endemicity

The level of endemicity, according to local conditions and defined based on the prevalence of malaria, may be holoendemic, hyperendemic, mesoendemic or hypoendemic. The definitions of these terms as they relate to malaria, and some of their characteristics, are as follows:

Holoendemic

Holoendemic means that high-level transmission occurs throughout the year. It is defined as having a prevalence constantly over 75% in children, but prevalence may be low in adults.
Hyperendemic

Hyperendemic means that transmission is most intense in the rainy season. It is defined as having a prevalence constantly over 50% in children, and also high prevalence in adults.
Mesoendemic

Mesoendemic means that transmission is more intense during the warmer months. Prevalence is between 11% and 50% in children.
Hypoendemic

Hypoendemic means that low-level transmission is intermittent throughout the year. Prevalence is at or below 10% in children.
