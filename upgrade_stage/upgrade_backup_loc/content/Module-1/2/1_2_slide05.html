Causes of Malaria

Malaria is caused by protozoan parasites of the genus Plasmodium. There are five species of malaria that infect humans namely:

  • 1. Plasmodium falciparum
  • 2. Plasmodium malariae
  • 3. Plasmodium vivax
  • 4. Plasmodium ovale (P. ovale curtisi and P. ovale wallikeri)
  • 5. Plasmodium knowlesi

In this course, you will focus on the diagnostic characteristics of the first four species. P. knowlesi infects monkeys in Southeast Asia and can be transmitted by infected mosquitoes to humans. P. knowlesi is morphologically similar to P. malariae but multiplies more rapidly and causes a more severe and potentially fatal disease.
