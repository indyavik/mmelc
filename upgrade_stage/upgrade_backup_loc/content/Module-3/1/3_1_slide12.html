Erythrocytes (10 of 10): Elliptocytosis

Elliptocytosis (also known as ovalocytosis) is a hereditary condition in which an unusually large number of erythrocytes are oval in shape rather than round. While an occasional oval-shaped cell is normal in a thin blood film, a large percentage can indicate elliptocytosis. These cells are referred to as elliptocytes or ovalocytes. Ovalocytosis can protect against P. vivax malaria.
