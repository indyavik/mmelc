Detecting Parasites for Case Management Screening and other Applications (2 of 3)

As malaria is a disease—a group of symptoms and signs caused by the presence of malaria parasites—diagnosis involves detecting parasites in a person with typical signs and symptoms of malaria and no other obvious cause of illness at a parasite density (count) that is sufficient to cause these symptoms.

It is well known that people can carry parasites in their blood without having significant symptoms–usually with very low parasite density, for example <5 parasites/ÂµL, well below the level at which symptoms normally occur. A test that detects such low densities of parasites in a person with no symptoms is better considered a parasite detection or parasite screening test, rather than a diagnostic test.
