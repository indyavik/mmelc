Lateral Flow Assays (6 of 10)

Figure 2 shows an example of an RDT and describes its use.

Figure 2: A typical lateral flow assay (cassette format). The blood specimen is applied to the sample well (S), which overlies the signal antibody pad. A chase buffer is then applied to the chase buffer well (B) and flows along with the sample and nanoparticle-labelled antibodies in the direction of the arrow through the nitrocellulose membrane over the test line (T) and the control line (C).

These lines appear only if or when the conjugated nanoparticles accumulate on the line. The sample and buffer continue flowing to the absorptive pad (P) which is hidden within the cartridge.
