Lateral Flow Assays (4 of 10)

The sensitivity of RDTs is limited by the concentration of antigen present in the blood specimen, and sometimes by other factors including the expression (production) of the target protein by the parasite. Protein production and form can vary, and in some countries populations of P. falciparum parasites that do not produce any HRP2 have been identified which are therefore not detectable by HRP2-detecting RDTs. In these situations, LDH tests or microscopy must be used.
