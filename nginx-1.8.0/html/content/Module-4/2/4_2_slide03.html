Lateral Flow Assays (Rapid Diagnostic Tests―RDTs)

Malaria RDTs (malaria lateral flow assays―LFAs) detect the presence of proteins (antigens) produced by the malaria parasite. RDTs are relatively cheap and simple to use, and generally have a similar threshold of detection (sensitivity) as routine microscopy. Because they do not require the skills and equipment required for microscopy, RDTs tend to be used towards the periphery of health systems where microscopy is more difficult to implement. In 2010, the World Health Organization recommended the use of either RDTs or microscopy for malaria diagnosis in all suspected non-severe malaria cases before treatment. By 2014, RDTs had overtaken microscopy as the most commonly used malaria diagnostic test.
