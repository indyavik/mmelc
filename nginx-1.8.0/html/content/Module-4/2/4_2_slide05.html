Lateral Flow Assays (3 of 10)

Depending on the antigen detected, RDTs can distinguish between one or more malaria parasite species. Many RDTs detect P. falciparum alone, while others also detect one or more of the other malaria parasite species. P. falciparum is normally targeted through detection of an antigen called histidine-rich protein-2 (HRP2).

Other tests target the lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) protein, which is present in P. falciparum and all the other parasite species. Versions of these tests can show the presence of any malaria parasite irrespective of species, or can distinguish between one or more species.
