Lateral Flow Assays (5 of 10)

A lateral flow assay is known as a sandwich assay. It uses antibodies to capture antigen (usually protein) from a sample, and bind it to a line on a nitrocellulose strip. The first (signal) antibody is bound (conjugated) to a minute particle (nanoparticle). If a sufficient quantity of the antibody-nanoparticle-antigen complex accumulates on the test line, the line becomes visible and the test is positive.

Most malaria RDTs are supplied as plastic cassettes—the nitrocellulose strip and absorbent pads are enclosed within the cassette, with windows for the test sample and a buffer to promote flow, and a window to allow the test and control lines to be viewed.
