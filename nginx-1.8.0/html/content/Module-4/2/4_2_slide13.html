Loop-Mediated Isothermal Amplification (LAMP)

While a number of NAAT tests exist apart from PCR, only one is currently commercially available for malaria diagnosis—the LAMP test. LAMP has advantages over PCR in that it requires only a single heating step (rather than repeated heating and cooling, or ‘thermocycling’), and it can be less affected by impurities in the sample. LAMP reactions amplify a very short strand of DNA, rapidly reaching a very high concentration.

The DNA is usually detected through by-products of the reaction, using fluorescence, turbidity, colour changes or light production. LAMP has certain disadvantages over PCR, in that it cannot easily be used to identify a range of variants (such as genotypes for drug resistance). LAMP has a potential role in settings where PCR is difficult to maintain, due to cost or complexity, but where highly-sensitive and specific parasite detection is required. LAMP is currently being used for screening in some malaria elimination programmes.
