Biological Causes of Poorly Prepared Blood Films

There are also biological factors that can lead to the preparation of poor blood films. You need to know these factors so that you can take corrective measures where possible. In cases where there are no solutions available, you need to appreciate these factors so that you do not unnecessarily reject blood films as being of poor quality. Biological factors that can cause poor preparation of blood films include cold agglutinins, lipaemia and rouleaux.




  • Cold Agglutinins
  • Lipaemia
  • Rouleaux

Cold agglutinins are antibodies that cause red blood cells (RBCs) to clump together at low temperatures. Red blood cells clump together forming microclots. This can be corrected by warming the blood to 37°C for five minutes and re-preparing the blood film.

Lipaemia refers to abnormally high concentrations of lipids in blood. Holes appear in blood films resembling a greasy glass slide. There is nothing you can do to correct this.

Rouleaux are stacks or aggregations of RBCs. RBCs resemble stacked coins in thin blood films. There is nothing you can do to correct this.
