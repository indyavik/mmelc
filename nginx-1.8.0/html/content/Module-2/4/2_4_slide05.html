Staining procedure for batch staining

Estimate the amount of 10% or 3% Giemsa stain working solution needed for the number of slides to be stained. Each slide needs approximately 3 mL of stain to cover it. Prepare the stain immediately before use as described previously.

Fix the thin blood films preferably using a Pasteur pipette to place 1–2 drops of absolute methanol on the thin blood film with the slide slanted at an angle to allow the methanol to spread; or by briefly dipping the thin blood film for 2 seconds in a small container or beaker containing absolute methanol. Avoid contact between the thick blood film and the methanol, as methanol and its vapour may fix the thick blood film which interferes with haemolysis of red blood cells.

Allow the methanol-fixed thin blood film to air-dry completely (approximately 2 minutes) by placing the slides on a flat surface. Never let the slides dry in a vertical position with the thin blood films facing down as this may cause the thick blood films to be fixed by the rising methanol vapour.

If using a Coplin jar or staining trough, arrange the slides vertically or horizontally according to the type of staining jar, with the thin films on the same side. If using a staining rack, place the sides on the rack with the blood films facing up. Allow reasonable space (approximately 1cm) between slides on the staining rack to prevent cross-contamination during staining. Handle the slides by their edges to avoid touching the blood films.

Pour the diluted Giemsa stain (10% or 3%) into the Coplin jar or staining trough by gently directing the flow of stain at one corner at the thin film end to avoid washing off the thick films; or gently pour the diluted stain on top of the slides on the staining rack at the thin film end. Do not pour stain directly onto the thick blood films, as they may float off the slide.



Set the timer for 8–10 minutes for 10% Giemsa stain solution or 45–60 minutes for 3% Giemsa stain solution to allow the blood films to stain. Experience with the stain you are using will help indicate the time needed for good staining.

After the staining time, gently wash the stain off the slides by adding buffered water gently from a measuring cylinder into the Coplin jar or staining trough at one corner, until all the stain has been washed away. Alternatively, gently add drops of buffered water to the slides on a staining rack until all the stain has been washed away. Do not pour the stain directly off the slides, as the metallic green surface scum will stick to the films, spoiling them for microscopy.

When the stain has been washed away, place the slides in a vertical position in the drying rack using forceps, the thick blood film down. Ensure the thick blood films do not scrape the edge of the rack.

Discard any remaining Giemsa stain working solution at the end of the day.
