Staining Procedure for batch staining

Estimate the amount of 10% or 3% Giemsa stain working solution needed for the number of slides to be stained. Each slide needs approximately 3 mL of stain to cover it. Prepare the stain immediately before use as described previously.

Fix the thin blood films preferably using a Pasteur pipette to place 1–2 drops of absolute methanol on the thin blood film, or by briefly dipping the thin blood film for 2 seconds in a small container or beaker containing absolute methanol. Avoid contact between the thick blood film and the methanol, as methanol and its vapour quickly fixes the thick blood film, and interferes with the haemolysis of the thick blood film.

Allow the methanol-fixed thin blood film to air-dry completely (approximately 2 minutes) by placing the slides on a flat surface. Never let the slides dry in a vertical position with the thin blood films down as this may cause the thick blood films to be fixed with methanol vapour.

Place the slides to be stained with the blood films facing down if using a Coplin jar or staining trough, or facing up if using a staining rack.

Pour the diluted Giemsa stain (10% or 3%) gently between the slide and the staining tray, if staining face down, until each slide is covered with stain, or gently pour the diluted stain onto the top of the slides lying face upwards on the staining rack. Do not pour the stain directly onto the thick blood films, as they may float off the slides.



Set the timer for 8–10 minutes for 10% Giemsa stain solution or 45–60 minutes for 3% Giemsa stain solution and allow the blood films to stain. Experience with the stain you are using will help indicate the time needed for good staining.

At the end of the staining time remove each slide individually. Gently flush the stain off the slide by adding drops of buffered water until all stain has been washed away. Do not pour the stain directly off the slides, as the metallic green surface scum will stick to the films, spoiling them for microscopy.

When the stain has been washed away, place the slides in the drying rack, film side downwards, or in vertical position with the thick blood film down to drain and dry. Ensure the thick blood films do not scrape the edge of the rack.

Discard any remaining Giemsa stain working solution at the end of the day.
