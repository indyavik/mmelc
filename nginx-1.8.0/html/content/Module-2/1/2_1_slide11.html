Wearing your laboratory coat and protective gloves, collect all the equipment and materials needed for the procedure and place them within easy reach on a tray or trolley. These include powder free gloves, lancets, sharps container, infectious waste bin, non-infectious waste bin, alcohol swabs and dry cotton swabs or gauze. Your SOPs should be close and visible, in case you need to refer to them.

Select the third finger from the thumb (ring finger) of the non-dominant hand (or big toe for infants, not the heel). Do not use the thumb in children or adults. You should avoid fingers with wounds, cuts, swellings, scars or disabilities.

Hold the patient's hand, palm facing upwards, and clean the selected finger with a piece of cotton wool soaked lightly in 70% methanol, or an alcohol swab. Advise patients with extremely dirty or greasy hands to first wash their hands with soap and water. Use firm strokes to remove dirt and oil from the ball of the finger and to stimulate blood circulation. Make sure the finger is warm by applying gentle massage if required. It is important to allow the alcohol to completely air dry.

Using a new, sterile lancet, puncture slightly off centre of the ball of the finger or toe. Immediately discard the lancet into the sharps container. Apply gentle pressure to the finger (or toe) and express the first drop of blood. Wipe the first drop of blood with dry cotton wool or gauze making sure that no cotton strands remain on the finger (or toe) that might stick to the blood.

All infectious waste (used gloves, alcohol swabs, dry alcohol swabs used to wipe the first drop of blood or used by the patient to stop the bleeding) should be discarded into the infectious waste bin. All other waste which is not infectious should be discarded into the non-infectious waste bin. You will learn how to make blood films for malaria diagnosis using finger prick blood in Unit Two.
