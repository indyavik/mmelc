Preparation of Giemsa Stain Working Solution for Malaria Microscopy (6 of 7)

Quality Control of Giemsa Stain and Buffered Distilled Water

It is important to observe the following:

  • You should prepare the Giemsa stain working solution before staining the blood film(s). You should also discard any excess diluted stain at the end of the day.
  • The quality of the stain and buffered water should be checked using a known positive malaria thin blood film. Blood films with P. vivax parasites are preferred to demonstrate the characteristic Schüffner’s dots. If not available, use a P. falciparum positive blood film and examine for Maurer’s clefts (if mature trophozoites are present).
  • The pH of the buffered distilled water and the correction fluids should be measured and recorded on a regular basis in the quality control logbook.
