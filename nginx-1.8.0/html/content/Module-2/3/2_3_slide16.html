Preparation of Buffered Distilled Water of pH 7.2

The procedure for preparation of buffered distilled water is described as follows:

Weigh 0.7 g potassium dihydrogen phosphate (KH2PO4).

Transfer the weighed KH2PO4 to the glass beaker, add about 150 mL distilled water and stir with a clean spatula until the salt dissolves.

Weigh 1 g disodium hydrogen phosphate (Na2HPO4) onto clean filter paper.

Add the Na2HPO4 to the solution in the beaker and stir as in Step Two.

When the salts have dissolved, add the solution to the conical flask and top up to the 1 L mark with distilled water.

Pour the buffered water into a glass bottle. Label clearly and document the procedure in the quality control logbook including the date of preparation, date of expiry, and name of the person who prepared the buffer as shown below.

Check the pH of the buffered water routinely before use. To adjust the pH, add small quantities of the correcting fluids to the buffer: 2% Na2HPO4 if the pH is below 7.2 (too acid) or 2% KH2PO4 if the pH is above 7.2 (too alkaline).
