Documents and Records (1 of 5)

Documents differ from records in the following ways:




  • Documents
  • Records

Documents provide policies and procedures to guide the work of the laboratory. Documents outline how to do things and require review and possible updating when a policy, process or procedure changes. Documents have standardised formats for recording and reporting information. Examples of documents include the quality manual, safety manual and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs).

Records provide the evidence that work is accomplished. They contain information that is either written by hand or computer-printed. Records are permanent and should not be updated, revised or modified. Examples of records include the following:

  • Patient reports
  • Daily quality control charts
  • Equipment maintenance logs
  • Quality control logs
  • Corrective action logs
  • External quality assessment (EQA) results
  • Personnel records
  • Completed patient satisfaction surveys
  • Signed internal audit reports
It is important to ensure that the records are readily accessible and retrievable but are protected from unauthorised alterations.
