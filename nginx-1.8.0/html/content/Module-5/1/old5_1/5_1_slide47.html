Process Improvement

The PDCA cycle is described as follows:




  • Plan
  • Do
  • Check
  • Act

Plan involves identifying problems or errors and their potential sources. This involves deciding on the steps to be used to gather information, such as asking questions to assess the current situation, analysing the root causes and using the gathered information to develop a plan for improvement.

Do involves implementing the plans you have developed, that is, putting the developed plan into action.

Check involves monitoring the effectiveness of the implemented action plan. This can be achieved through reviews and audit processes. After 'checking', you may require to revise your action plan to achieve the improvements that you need.

Act involves taking the required corrective action and then re-checking to make sure the solution has worked.
