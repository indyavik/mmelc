Information Management (3 of 3)

In the process of managing your laboratory information management system, you should be aware of the following problems and how to avoid them:

  • Incomplete or illegible results for test interpretation.
  • Inadequate patient identification systems.
  • Forms that are inadequately designed to meet laboratory and user needs.
  • Standardised forms prepared by others that may not be suitable for your laboratoryâ€™s needs.
  • Inability to retrieve data due to poor archiving processes or insufficient backup of computerised information.
  • Poor data organisation, which may hinder later data analysis to meet research or other needs.
  • Incompatibility between computerised information systems and other electronic systems, resulting in problems with data transmission.
