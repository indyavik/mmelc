Document and Record Control Plan

Documents by definition require regular review and may require updating. In your laboratory, you must establish a system for managing documents so that current versions are always available. A document control system provides procedures for formatting and maintaining an inventory of documents and should include the following:

  • Maintaining a master list of all documents in the laboratory.
  • Ensuring that the most current version of any document is the one that is in use.
  • Ensuring availability of documents when needed.
  • Having a uniform format for documents that includes a numbering system and a method for identifying the version (date) of the document.
  • Having a process for formal approval of documents, a distribution plan and a procedure for updating and revising documents.
