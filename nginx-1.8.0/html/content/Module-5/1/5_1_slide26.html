pH Meter

A pH meter is used to measure acidity or alkalinity of solutions using electrodes. It is important to note that the membrane and diaphragm of the electrodes can become contaminated which can result in measurement errors or slow electrode response. Therefore, adopting regular preventative maintenance procedures will help to reduce or eliminate any errors.

The following are important preventative care and maintenance procedures for the pH meter:

  • At the end of each day of use, wipe the surface of the pH meter with a soft cloth soaked in 70% methanol.
  • Soak the electrode in 0.1 M hydrochloric acid (HCl) for five minutes. Remove and rinse well with de-ionised water and soak in 0.1 M sodium hydroxide (NaOH) for five minutes. Remove, rinse again with de-ionised water and store in pH electrode storage solution (pH 4 buffer is suitable for short term storage).
