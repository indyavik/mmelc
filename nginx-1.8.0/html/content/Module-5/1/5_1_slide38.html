Documents and Records (3 of 5)

SOP Format

A good SOP should contain the following elements:

Elements of an SOP format

Document number, page numbers and number of pages, version number, effective date, and distribution sites.

Title—including name of test procedure or process in the testing cycle, type of specimen, and method of testing.

Principle and purpose.

Specimen requirements and collection methods.

Specimen handling and preparation to include reagents, instrumentation and detailed information on the entire testing process, including pre-examination, examination and post-examination phases.

  • Pre-examination instructions should address patient identification, specimen collection, labelling, and transport to the laboratory.
  • Examination instructions should address the actual step-by-step laboratory procedures and quality control procedures needed to ensure accuracy and reliability of results.
  • Post-examination instructions should provide information on reporting of results using the correct reporting format, instructions on how to deal with urgent or life-threatening results (sometimes called ‘panic values’), and transmission or communication of results to end users.

Limitations and notes

Name of the person preparing the SOP

Approval signatures and dates

Document change history
