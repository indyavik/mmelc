Documents and Records (1 of 5)

Documents differ from records in the following ways:




  • Documents
  • Records

Documents provide policies and procedures to guide the work of the laboratory. Documents outline how to do things and require review and possible updating when a policy, process or procedure changes. Examples of documents include the Quality Manual, Safety Manual and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs).

Records provide the evidence that work is accomplished. Records are permanent and do not require updating, revision or modification. Examples of records include:

  • Signed patient reports
  • External quality assessment (EQA) results, such as performance in Proficiency Testing (PT) or slide rechecking programmes
  • Completed patient satisfaction surveys
  • Signed internal audit reports
