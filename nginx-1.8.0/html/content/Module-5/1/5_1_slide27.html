Equipment Management (7 of 7)

Pipettes

Pipettes are devices used to measure small volumes of liquids. In malaria microscopy, pipettes are used to measure specified volumes of blood when preparing blood films, that is, 6 µL for thick blood films and 2 µL for thin blood films.

For consistency of volumes, perform the following preventive maintenance procedures routinely on pipettes:

  • Check the pipette at the beginning of each day for dirt and dust. To clean a dirty pipette, wipe the surface with a sponge moistened with 70% methanol.
  • If the pipette is contaminated, decontaminate the outside of the pipette by wiping with 70% methanol. Allow the methanol to remain on the pipette for at least 10 minutes and then dry the pipette with lint-free wipes.
  • Visually inspect the general appearance of the pipette for worn and cracked parts. If not in use, the pipette should always be stored in the upright position to prevent residual liquid from entering the tip cone.
  • If the pipette is used daily, it should be cleaned and relevant parts lubricated at least every three months. Lubrication should only be performed by a trained person or biomedical engineer following the manufacturer’s instructions in the user manual.
  • Calibrate and verify the performance of the pipette by checking for leaks. A leaking pipette may indicate a loose or wrong sized tip, a contaminated or corroded piston, or a worn seal.
