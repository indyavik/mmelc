Phases of Errors in Malaria Microscopy (3 of 4)

Examination Errors

A list of common errors that occur during the testing process include:

  • Failing to follow an established procedure for staining and microscopic examination of blood films
  • Reporting results when internal quality control checks for Giemsa stain or RDT kits have failed
  • Incorrect use of malaria microscopy reagents for staining blood specimens, usually due to dilution or pipetting errors
  • Incorrect use of RDT kits
  • Using reagents or RDT kits that have been improperly stored
  • Using expired reagents or RDT kits
  • Using defective equipment such as a microscope with a damaged x100 objective lens
  • Over-staining or under-staining of blood films
  • Failing to enter the complete results into the laboratory register, which limits future tracking of results
