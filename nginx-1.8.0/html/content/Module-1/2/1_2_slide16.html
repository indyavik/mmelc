Some antimalarial drugs are contra-indicated in pregnancy, such as primaquine and artesunate-based preparations (in the first trimester). Primaquine is also contraindicated in individuals with glucose 6-phospate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency, a congenital enzyme deficiency in red cells. G6PD maintains haemoglobin in a functioning state and acute haemolysis may occur when red cells that are G6PD deficient are exposed to certain drugs, such as primaquine. A screening test can be performed to detect G6PD deficiency in an individual before primaquine is given.
