Life Cycle of Malaria (5 of 5)

Gametocytes occur in much lower densities in the blood stream than trophozoites so are not commonly seen in most peripheral blood films. However, they play a critical role in malaria transmission as their presence in blood constitutes a reservoir of infection for the mosquito vectors, even when they cannot be detected by microscopy. A person with gametocytes in the blood stream is therefore a ‘malaria carrier’.

With long practice, a microscopist can identify most malaria parasite species and stages almost on sight.
