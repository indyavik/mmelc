Life Cycle of Malaria (4 of 5)

P. vivax and P. ovale are also able to produce dormant schizonts in the liver known as hypnozoites that remain in the liver cells and can cause relapses weeks or years after apparent cure.

P. falciparum and P. malariae infections may also recur after inadequate treatment and apparent cure; in these cases, the parasite remains in undetectably low levels in the blood and later may increase in density and cause symptoms, a process known as recrudescence. In the case of P. malariae, recrudescence may occur decades later.
