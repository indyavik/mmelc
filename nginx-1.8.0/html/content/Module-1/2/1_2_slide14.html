Malaria Symptoms and Signs in Relation to the Parasite Life Cycle (2 of 3)

Stages in the Malaria Life Cycle in Relation to Clinical Features

The period between the injection of sporozoites into the blood stream by the mosquito until rupture of red blood cells by erythrocytic schizonts is the incubation period. During the incubation period there are no symptoms or signs of malaria. There is no fever when sporozoites are present in blood and during the liver phase of the malaria parasite life cycle. The incubation period is typically between 7 and 30 days, and varies with malaria parasite species. P. falciparum typically has a shorter incubation period, while P. malariae is most likely to have a longer incubation period.

Fever occurs when red blood cells are ruptured by mature schizonts and merozoites are released into the blood stream. Since released merozoites re-enter red blood cells and restart the cycle, fever in untreated patients may occur in cycles. Circulating gametocytes, which appear some days after the appearance of asexual forms on microscope slides in P. falciparum infection but may occur simultaneously in P. vivax infection, do not cause fever.

The main consequences of malaria infection are anaemia caused by destruction of infected erythrocytes (red blood cells) and organ failure due to the effects of parasite adherence (P. falciparum-infected erythrocytes) to capillaries.
