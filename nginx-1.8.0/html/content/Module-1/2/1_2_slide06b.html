Definition of Terminologies Relevant to Malaria Parasites (4 of 4)

Description of Terms

The terms below describe the life stages of the malaria parasite, which will be covered in more detail in the following pages:

• Sporozoite: Greek, sporos = seed; zoon = animal. This is the infective stage that the mosquito injects into the human host. Sporozoites are found in the salivary glands of the infected female mosquito.

• Schizont: Greek, schizein = to divide. This is the developmental stage of the malaria parasite in the liver and blood, that divides asexually. A schizont is composed of a number of merozoites.

• Hypnozoite: Greek, hypnos = sleep. This is the dormant stage in the liver that occurs in P. vivax and P. ovale infections.

• Merozoite: Greek, meros = part. This is the stage formed by asexual division of a schizont or hypnozoite.

• Trophozoite: Greek, trophein = to feed. This stage actively feeds and grows inside parasitised erythrocytes and develops into schizonts or gametocytes.

• Gametocyte: Greek, gametes = spouse; kystis = bladder. "Male" microgametocytes or "female" macrogametocytes are found in the blood and are ingested by a biting female Anopheles mosquito. The microgametocyte further divides to form flagellated microgametes; these continue the sexual stage of the parasite within the gut of the mosquito.

• Ookinete: Greek, oon = egg; kinesis = motion. This is the motile stage within the mosquito resulting from fertilisation of a female macrogamete by a male microgamete.

• Oocyst: Greek, oon = egg; kystis = bladder. This is the developmental stage within the midgut of the mosquito in which sporozoites develop and are eventually released.

• Anopheles: Greek, an = without; ophelos = benefit. Thus, harmful, not useful.
