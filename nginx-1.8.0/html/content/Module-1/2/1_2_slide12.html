Diagnostic Algorithms Related to Malaria Parasite Stages

Algorithms can be adopted or designed to help with diagnosis, and a simple one is presented here, assuming one is examining a Giemsa-stained thin blood film.

Thick blood films are better for detecting parasites; thin blood films are better for determining species and stage. Detection and identification of malaria parasites will be covered in detail in Module Three.

In all cases, species should be identified only after examining several parasite stages in a blood film. You will be provided with this algorithm again in Module Three, where you can read through the descriptions and use the virtual slides provided to compare the findings.

The following table summarises the findings for erythrocyte examinations, which will be revisited in Module Three:

  1. Erythrocytes contain inclusions with red nucleus and blue cytoplasm.
    Think: malaria present
  2. Erythrocytes are normal size with delicate ring-shaped parasites, other stages rare or absent.
    Think P. falciparum
  3. Erythrocyte stretched by crescentic or banana-shaped parasite.
    Think P. falciparum
  4. Erythrocytes are normal size with more robust or compact ring-shaped parasites, several other stages present.
    Think P. malariae
  5. Erythrocytes are enlarged, stippled, other stages present.
    Think P. vivax
  6. Erythrocytes are enlarged, oval, stippled, fimbriated, other stages present.
    Think P. ovale
