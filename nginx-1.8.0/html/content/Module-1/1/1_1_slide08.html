Local Endemicity

The level of endemicity, according to local conditions and defined based on the prevalence of malaria, may be holoendemic, hyperendemic, mesoendemic or hypoendemic. Holoendemic

Holoendemic is defined as having a prevalence constantly over 75% in children, but prevalence may be low in adults. High-level transmission occurs throughout the year.
Hyperendemic

Hyperendemic is defined as having a prevalence constantly over 50% in children, and also high prevalence in adults. Transmission may be seasonal, being most intense in the rainy season.
Mesoendemic

Mesoendemic is defined as having a prevalence between 10% and 50% in children. Transmission may be more intense during the warmer months.
Hypoendemic

Hypoendemic means that prevalence is at or below 10% in children. Low-level transmission is intermittent throughout the year.
