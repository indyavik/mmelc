Malaria Control Strategies (2 of 5)

Other personal interventions that can be implemented to prevent and control malaria include the following:

  • 1. Where conditions are appropriate: Chemoprevention for the most vulnerable populations including Intermittent Preventive Treatment in Pregnancy (IPTp); Intermittent Preventive Treatment in Children less than 12 months of age (IPTc); and Seasonal Malaria Chemoprevention (SMC) in children less than six years of age.
  • 2. In all cases: Confirmation of malaria diagnosis through microscopy or malaria Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDT) for every suspected case before treatment.
  • 3. In all cases: Timely treatment of confirmed cases with appropriate antimalarial medicines according to parasite species and documented drug resistance.
