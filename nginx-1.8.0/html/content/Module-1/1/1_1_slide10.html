Global Epidemiology of Malaria (7 of 8)

Local Endemicity

In areas of stable malaria, anaemia is common and mortality is highest in children under two years of age. Asymptomatic infection may still occur in persistent, inadequately treated infections.

In areas where malaria is periodic, little immunity develops within the population and mortality occurs more consistently across all age groups, including adults. This is sometimes known as unstable malaria.

Understanding these considerations is vital for developing effective control programmes to reduce a population's exposure to infection, address mortality and potentially interrupt transmission. Therefore, malaria control programmes must be supported by rigorous and sustained surveillance.
