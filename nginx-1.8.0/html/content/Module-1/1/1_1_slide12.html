Malaria Control Strategies (1 of 5)

An understanding of the global malaria context will help you describe appropriate strategies for malaria control in your country or region. This is particularly important because malaria is a completely preventable disease.

The following personal and environmental interventions can be implemented to prevent and control malaria:

  • 1. Vector control through the use of Indoor Residual Spraying (IRS) and in some specific settings, larval control.
  • 2. Personal protection through use of Insecticide Treated Nets (ITN), which also has a wider benefit in reducing vector populations.
  • 3. Personal protection through use of repellent and insecticidal sprays, coils, and wearing long clothing after dark.
