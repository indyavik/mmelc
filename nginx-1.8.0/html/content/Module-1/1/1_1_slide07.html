Global Epidemiology of Malaria (4 of 8)

Local Endemicity

Malaria transmission depends on environmental and climatic factors such as temperature and rainfall. The malaria parasite cannot develop in the mosquito at temperatures below 18oC, and thrives at temperatures above 22oC. Thus, even in malaria endemic countries, transmission decreases at higher altitudes and is not usually found at altitudes above 1,600 metres.

Plasmodium falciparum accounts for the majority of malaria infections globally; some species of malaria, such as Plasmodium vivax, may be more prevalent in cooler regions because it has greater tolerance to lower ambient temperatures. Transmission may also be seasonal depending on rainfall and other climatic factors.

It is expected that the areas compatible with malaria transmission will increase with global warming in the coming years.
