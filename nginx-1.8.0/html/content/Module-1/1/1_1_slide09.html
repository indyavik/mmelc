Global Epidemiology of Malaria (6 of 8)

Local Endemicity

In areas of high malaria prevalence, where most of the population may be infected, individuals develop a high degree of immunity to malaria, and malaria infection in older children and adults may be asymptomatic or cause only mild disease.

In these settings, known as stable malaria, the most vulnerable groups are young children who have not yet developed immunity to malaria; and pregnant women, whose immunity is decreased by pregnancy.
