Background (1 of 2)

In 2008, the World Health Organization's (WHO) Roll Back Malaria (RBM) Partnership unveiled a global framework describing short, medium and long-term goals for malaria control, elimination and eventual eradication. The plan comprises a three-part strategy:

  • Aggressive control to achieve low transmission and mortality in 61 tropical countries with the highest burdens of malaria
  • Progressive elimination of malaria from endemic margins inward to shrink the malaria map
  • Research to find a vaccine and better drugs, diagnostics, insecticides and other tools for better management and control of malaria
