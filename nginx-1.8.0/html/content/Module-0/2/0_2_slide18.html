Question 5: The thick blood film is preferable to the thin blood film for:
Correct Answer: Counting malaria parasites.
You answered correctly.
Thick blood films contain several layers of red cells, whereas thin blood films contain a single layer of spread red cells. Thus, for a fixed number of microscope fields, thick blood films allow the microscopist to examine a larger number of red cells for the presence of parasites, and low parasitaemias can be more readily identified in a thick blood film.
