Question 1: Malaria transmission that is most intense in the rainy season is characteristic of regions where malaria is:

Question 2: The most effective stain used in the microscopic diagnosis of malaria in a thin blood film is:
Correct Answer: Giemsa stain.
You answered correctly.
Giemsa stain produces very good red-blue differentiation which facilitates the detection and identification of malaria parasites.

Question 3: The thin blood film should be fixed by:
Correct Answer: Immersing in absolute methanol.
You answered correctly.
Rapid immersion in absolute methanol is required to preserve the morphology of the blood components and parasites.

Question 4: The primary role of platelets is:
Correct Answer: Clotting of blood.
You answered correctly.
Haemostasis, or clotting of blood, is the primary function of platelets.
