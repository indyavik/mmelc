Plasmodium falciparum (1 of 3)

Plasmodium falciparum is the most common species of malaria to infect humans and is responsible for most severe and fatal cases of malaria. Most commonly, P. falciparum is observed in blood films as young, ring-stage trophozoite parasites, but later stages can also be observed. P. falciparum-infected erythrocytes are normal sized and may contain Maurer's clefts.
