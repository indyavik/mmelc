Malaria Parasite Species (2 of 6)

This module will focus on the diagnostic characteristics of the first four species. P. knowlesi occurs in monkeys in Southeast Asia, but can be transmitted by infected mosquitoes to humans. P. knowlesi is morphologically similar to P. malariae, and cannot be identified using microscopy alone.

It is essential to determine accurately the species of malaria for effective treatment. There are currently a limited number of drugs used to treat malaria. Most of these target the blood stages of the parasites so eliminating P. falciparum and P. malariae parasites from the blood is sufficient for cure.

The treatment of P. vivax and P. ovale requires the addition of a tissue schizonticide, such as primaquine, to eliminate the hypnozoite, the dormant liver stage of these species. P. falciparum and P. knowlesi infections can lead to rapidly fatal disease and must be treated without delay.
