Mixed Malaria Infections

Mixed malaria infections occur when more than one species of malaria is present in a single blood film. Mixed malaria infections are recognised by the presence of stages that are unique to the different species of Plasmodium parasites. For example, there may be a crescentic P. falciparum gametocyte in the same blood film as a mature trophozoite of P. vivax showing an amoeboid, vacuolated parasite in a stippled, enlarged and distorted erythrocyte.

Similarly, a P. falciparum gametocyte may be observed as well as an enlarged, compact, vacuolated trophozoite in an ovoid, stippled cell with projections at the narrow ends characteristic of P. ovale. Differences in early trophozoite morphology should not be used to indicate a mixed infection because early trophozoites of all species are often similar. The rosette arrangement of 6–8 merozoites associated with the schizont of P. malariae can also be observed in other species.
