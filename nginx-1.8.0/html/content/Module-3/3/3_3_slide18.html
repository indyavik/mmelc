Plasmodium ovale (1 of 3)

Plasmodium ovale is relatively rare, but is observed in many regions of the world. It is most common in West Africa, but is also present in the Lake Victoria region of East Africa and Papua New Guinea. All stages may be observed in a blood film. P. ovale-infected erythrocytes are normal to slightly enlarged, are occasionally ovoid (oval-shaped) with fimbriations (fringe-like cytoplasm), and contain abundant stippling with coarse James' dots.
