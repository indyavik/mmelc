Algorithm for Species Identification

In Module One, you were presented with a brief algorithm that the microscopist can use to identify malaria parasite species. The algorithm is presented again here, and you are encouraged to think about this algorithm as it pertains to the images and descriptions you have covered in this unit. As you go through the practical exercises, keep this algorithm in mind.

  1. Erythrocytes contain inclusions with red nucleus and blue cytoplasm.
    Think: malaria present
  2. Erythrocytes are normal size with delicate ring-shaped parasites, other stages rare or absent.
    Think P. falciparum
  3. Erythrocyte stretched by crescentic or banana-shaped parasite.
    Think P. falciparum
  4. Erythrocytes are normal size with more robust or compact ring-shaped parasites, several other stages present.
    Think P. malariae
  5. Erythrocytes are enlarged, stippled, other stages present.
    Think P. vivax
  6. Erythrocytes are enlarged, oval, stippled, fimbriated, other stages present.
    Think P. ovale
