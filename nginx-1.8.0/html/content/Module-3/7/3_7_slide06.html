Laboratory Records

The result of the blood film examination must be recorded in the laboratory register, which stays in the laboratory. The table that follows is an example of a laboratory register. The register is part of the laboratory data management system. Once again, if your laboratory register is missing any critical information it should be modified.

As discussed in Unit Two, samples in which parasites are not observed are reported as 'No Malaria Parasites Seen' or NMPS, rather than 'Negative for malaria parasites'.

As a reminder, at least 100 oil immersion fields of a thick blood film should be examined before reporting 'No malaria parasites seen'.
