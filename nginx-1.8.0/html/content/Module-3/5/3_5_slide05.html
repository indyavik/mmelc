Borrelia in Blood Films (1 of 2)

Borrelia is a genus of spirochaetes (spiral bacteria) that cause relapsing fever in humans which can lead to meningoencephalitis and fatal disease. The causative organisms are B. crocidurae in West Africa, and B. duttoni and B. recurrentis in eastern Africa.

Borrelia organisms are transmitted by ticks or body lice. The disease can present with similar symptoms to malaria, but requires antibiotic treatment for cure. In stained blood films, Borrelia organisms present as faintly staining spirals about 20 Âµm long.

B. burgdorferi, B. afzelli, and B. garinii cause Lyme disease, a tick-borne disease which presents with skin rash and joint pain. Lyme disease, which is most common in North America, Europe and Asia, is usually diagnosed by detecting antibodies in blood rather than detecting the bacteria directly in blood films.
