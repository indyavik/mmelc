Trypanosomes in Blood Films

Trypanosomes are flagellated blood and tissue-dwelling protozoa that can invade the central nervous system. Trypanosoma brucei species cause African sleeping sickness and are transmitted by biting flies of the genus Glossina (tsetse flies). Trypanosoma cruzi causes Chagas disease, most common in South America, Central America and Mexico. Chagas disease is transmitted by the triatomine or ‘kissing’ bug (Triatoma infestans).

Trypanosomes are up to 30 µm long, with a central nucleus, a posterior kinetoplast, and an anterior flagellum attached to an undulating membrane that extends most of the length of the organism. Trypanosomes can occasionally be mistaken for malaria trophozoites or P. falciparum gametocytes. The images below illustrate Trypanosoma brucei spp. and Trypanosoma cruzi parasites in blood films.
