Errors in Blood Film Preparation (4 of 6)

Improper pH of Stain or Buffer

Giemsa stain should be prepared to a pH of 7.2. An acidic or basic (alkaline) stain can distort the resulting colour of erythrocytes, leucocytes and the nucleus and cytoplasm of parasites. The acidic stain leads to red erythrocytes and more faintly defined parasite cytoplasm. The basic stain leads to blue/grey erythrocytes and lack of contrast between parasite nucleus and cytoplasm. Despite this, malaria trophozoites can still be clearly identified.
