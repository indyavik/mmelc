Errors in Blood Film Preparation (2 of 6)

Fixed Thick Blood Film

This mistake can occur in the following circumstances:

  • Fixing the thick blood film while fixing the thin blood film. Extreme care must be taken when fixing the thin blood film to ensure the thick blood film is not also fixed. The thick blood film may be inadvertently dipped in methanol or may be fixed by methanol vapours while fixing the thin blood film. This can be prevented by tilting the slide with the sample side up, so methanol vapour does not pass over the thick blood film as the methanol evaporates.
  • Use of excessive heat from an electric hair-dryer or heating block, or use of direct sunlight to dry the films. Therefore direct heat should never be used to dry blood films.
  • A thick blood film that is not stained shortly after making the film can become fixed over time (autofixation).
