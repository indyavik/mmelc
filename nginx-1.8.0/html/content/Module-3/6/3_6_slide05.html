Errors in Blood Film Preparation (1 of 6)

Several common sample preparation mistakes can also make diagnosis based on blood film examination difficult or impossible. While these mistakes are covered in detail in Module Two, here you will be presented with some of the most common mistakes and the resulting appearance of blood films. By following the proper technique, including using fresh, high quality reagents and new or properly cleaned slides, these mistakes can be avoided.

Fixed Thick Blood Film

The thick blood film should not be fixed prior to staining, as the erythrocytes must be lysed for the film to be read. It is not possible to observe parasites in a fixed thick blood film, as seen in the images below:
