Errors in Blood Film Preparation (3 of 6)

Unfixed or Under-Fixed Thin Blood Films

It is also possible that a thin blood film is not fixed or is under-fixed. Thin blood films must be fixed before staining so the blood cells retain their morphology. The most common cause of this is simply forgetting to fix the thin blood film. In this case, the thin blood film may wash away completely during staining.

Under-fixing may be caused by using low quality or hydrated methanol (methanol contaminated with water).
