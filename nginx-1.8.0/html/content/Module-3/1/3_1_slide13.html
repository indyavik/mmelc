Leucocyte Function and Morphology

Leucocytes defend the body against infectious agents by ingesting them or by producing specific antibodies. Leucocytes are variable in size, measuring 7–15 Î¼m in diameter, and contain large nuclei that appear dark red in stained preparations.

There are two classes of leucocytes namely:

  • Granulocytes; which include neutrophils, eosinophils and basophils.
  • Non-granulocytes; which include monocytes and lymphocytes.
