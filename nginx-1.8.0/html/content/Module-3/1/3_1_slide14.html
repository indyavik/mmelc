Leucocytes (2 of 11): Morphology

These different types of leucocytes have specific morphology and staining characteristics, which are well preserved in thin blood films so they can be readily identified. Leucocytes are not lysed in thick blood films and are also easily recognised, although they often appear distorted and the leucocyte types may not be easily identified.

Recognising leucocytes in a blood film is important for quantitating malaria parasites (described in Unit Four), for an overall assessment of leucocyte density (increased or decreased) and for identifying abnormalities in leucocyte appearance, which may suggest the presence of a disease other than malaria.
