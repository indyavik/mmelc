Erythrocytes (3 of 10): Morphology

Occasionally, erythrocytes in a thin blood film may be crenated. Crenation is the formation of projections of the erythrocyte membrane when the cell loses volume due to ingestion of its contents by a malaria parasite, or when the cell is exposed to a hypertonic (highly salty) solution. Because the cell membrane does not shrink, it produces folds which are seen as projections.
