Leucocytes (6 of 11): Neutrophil abnormalities

In bacterial infections, neutrophils may contain vacuoles or coarse granules (‘toxic granulations’). While granules are seen in neutrophils from a healthy patient, numerous coarse, dark granules in neutrophils may indicate bacterial infection in the patient. Other indications of bacterial infection are vacuoles and Döhle bodies, which appear as faint blue or grey bodies.
