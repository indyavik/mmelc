Erythrocytes (9 of 10): Sickle Cell Disease

Various congenital (inherited) abnormalities of haemoglobin or red cell membrane structure cause changes in the appearance of red blood cells. Sickle cell disease occurs in people of African origin and results in curved and rigid red cells that are poor carriers of oxygen and may block capillaries. Sickled cells can be observed in a thin blood film. Sickle cell trait (inheritance of one sickle cell gene) confers some resistance to the severe effects of malaria disease.
