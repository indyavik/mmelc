Erythrocytes (1 of 10): Function

You will start by describing the function and morphology of erythrocytes (red blood cells) before addressing common abnormalities and their clinical significance.

Erythrocyte Function

Erythrocytes are the most numerous cells in blood, with a range of 4–6 million per microlitre. Red blood cells contain haemoglobin that transports oxygen and carbon dioxide around the body and gives blood its red colour.

Erythrocytes are the host cells of the malaria parasite in the erythrocytic stage of the parasite cycle (outside the liver).

Red blood cells are only seen in thin blood films, when the blood film is fixed before staining. In a properly prepared thick blood film, erythrocytes are lysed and are not visible, although diffuse erythrocyte stroma (cellular material) may be seen.
