Erythrocytes (6 of 10): B12 and Folic Acid Deficiency Anaemia

Lack of folic acid or vitamin B12 results in large red cells (oval macrocytes), and in severe cases nucleated red blood cells appear in the circulation. Nucleated red blood cells are immature red blood cells that can be seen in blood films from neonates for up to five days after birth; however, in children and adults, nucleated red blood cells are normally only seen in films of bone marrow and their presence in a peripheral blood film may indicate anaemia (such as due to folic acid or vitamin B12 deficiency), other bone marrow disorders or malignant tumours invading the bone.
