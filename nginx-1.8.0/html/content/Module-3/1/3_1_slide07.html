Erythrocytes (5 of 10): Iron Deficiency Anaemia

The most common erythrocyte disorder results from insufficient iron for production of haemoglobin (iron deficiency anaemia). Lack of iron results in small red blood cells with an enlarged area of central pallor (hypochromic, microcytic cells). Red blood cells of a patient with iron deficiency anaemia may also show variation in size (anisocytosis) and shape (poikilocytosis).
