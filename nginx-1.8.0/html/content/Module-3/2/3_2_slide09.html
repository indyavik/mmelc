General Morphological Features of Malaria Parasites (1 of 2)

The morphological features common to each stage and all species of malaria parasites are the nucleus which stains dark red and is refractile (shiny); the cytoplasm which stains blue; a vacuole which appears as a clear space within the cytoplasm; and malaria pigment which may be brown or black. The vacuole may not be apparent in gametocytes.

The two critical features for identification of malaria parasites are the nucleus and the cytoplasm.
